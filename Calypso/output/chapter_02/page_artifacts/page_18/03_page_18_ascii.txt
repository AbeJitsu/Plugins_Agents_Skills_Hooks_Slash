│  Classifications of property.                                              │
│                                                                             │
│  [Body Text - 11pt Regular with italics]                                   │
│  Our legal system recognizes two classifications of property: real         │
│  property and personal property. Real property is ownership of real        │
│  estate and the bundle of rights associated with owning the real          │
│  estate. Personal property is ownership of anything which is not real      │
│  estate, and the rights associated with owning the personal property      │
│  item. Items of personal property are also called chattels or             │
│  personalty.                                                               │
│                                                                             │
│  [Note paragraph - 11pt Regular with italics]                              │
│  Note: since all real estate in the United States is owned by some        │
│  person, private organization, or government entity, all real estate      │
│  in the country is real property. Given that fact, this text will         │
│  follow the customary practice of using the two terms interchangeably     │
│  and synonymously.                                                         │
│                                                                             │

│                                                                             │
│  [Body Text - 11pt Regular with bold terms]                                │

│                                                                             │
│  [Body Text - 11pt Regular]                                                │
│  Real property rights consist of the bundle of rights associated with     │
│  owning a parcel of real estate. Foremost of these rights is the right    │
│  of possession.                                                             │
│                                                                             │
│  [Body Text with italics - 11pt Regular]                                   │
│  The right to use a property refers to the right to use it in certain     │
│  ways, such as mining, cultivating, landscaping, razing, and building     │
│  on the property. The right is subject to the limitations of local        │
│  zoning and the legality of the use. One's right to use may not          │
│  infringe on the rights of others to use and enjoy their property. For    │
│  example, an owner may be restricted from constructing a large pond on    │
│  her property if in fact the pond would pose flooding and drainage        │
│  hazards to the next door neighbor.                                       │
