Ownership in severalty is the exclusive ownership of real property by one person. The fee simple absolute estate is ownership in severalty. The property owner has absolute control of all bundle rights.

Tenancy in common

The tenancy in common, also known as the estate in common, is the most common form of co-ownership when the owners are not married. The characteristics are:

  • two or more owners
  • identical rights
  • interests individually owned
  • no survivorship
  • may transfer interest without consent of other owners
  • may dispose by will

Each co-owner holds separate title to the property. When a co-owner dies, that owner's interest passes to the deceased owner's heirs or devisees, not to the other co-owners.
