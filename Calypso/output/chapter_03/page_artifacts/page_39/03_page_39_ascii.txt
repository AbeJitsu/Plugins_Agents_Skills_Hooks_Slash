│  [Body Text - 11pt Regular]                                                │
│  The tenancy in common, also known as the estate in common, is the most    │
│  common form of co-ownership when the owners are not married. The          │
│  characteristics are:                                                       │
│                                                                             │
│  [Bullet List - 11pt Regular]                                              │
│  ● two or more owners                                                       │
│  ● identical rights                                                         │
│  ● interests individually owned                                             │
│  ● no survivorship                                                          │
│  ● may transfer interest without consent of other owners                    │
│  ● may dispose by will                                                      │
│                                                                             │
│  [Body Text - 11pt Regular]                                                │
│  Each co-owner holds separate title to the property. When a co-owner dies, │
│  that owner's interest passes to the deceased owner's heirs or devisees,   │
