The exhibit shows three parties, A, B and C, who acquired a property as joint tenants. By definition, each owns a one-third share. If C sells his interest to D, A and B automatically become joint tenants of two-thirds of the property, and D becomes a tenant in common with A and B. D's interest will pass to her heirs, not to A and B.

If C dies, A and B receive equal shares of C's estate, making their shares equal 50%. If B then dies, A acquires the whole estate and becomes the sole owner. This event terminates the joint tenancy estate, and it becomes property in severalty.

Creation of joint tenancy

To create a joint tenancy, all owners must acquire the property at the same time, use the same deed, acquire equal shares, and share in equal rights of possession. These are referred to as the "four unities"

unity of time

All joint tenants must acquire their interests in the property at the same time.

unity of title

All joint tenants must acquire their interests by the same deed or will.

unity of interest

Each joint tenant must have an identical interest (share) in the property.

unity of possession

Each joint tenant must have an equal right of possession of the whole property.
