Calculating Area

To find the area of an irregular shape, divide it into triangles, rectangles, squares or trapezoids and calculate the areas of those parts.

Area of a Square or Rectangle
  
1. Formula:  
Area formula: a=b×h
2. Example: A square measures 3 feet on each side.
Calculation: 3×3=9 SF
Area of a Triangle
  
1. Formula:  
Triangle area formula: a=b×h2
2. Example: A triangle has a 20' base and a 4' height.
Calculation: a=20×42=40 SF
Area of a Trapezoid
  
1. Formula:  
Trapezoid area formula: a=h(b1+b2)2
2. Example: A trapezoid's two bases are 10' and 15', and its height is 7'.
Calculation: a=7(10+15)2=87.5 SF

Investments (Chapter 18): Appreciation

Appreciation

  
1. Formulas:  
Total Appreciation: current−original
Rate: appreciationoriginal
2. Example: House bought for $500,000 appreciates $50,000 per year for 3 years  
Total: $650,000−$500,000=$150,000
Rate: $150,000$500,000=30%

Investments: Equity Calculation

Equity Calculation

  
1. Formula:  
Equity formula: equity=current value−current loan
2. Example: Property bought for $600,000 with $450,000 loan, appreciates $60,000, loan reduced by $30,000  
Calculation: ($600,000+$60,000)−($450,000−$30,000)=$240,000

Investments: Return Calculations

Return Calculations

  
1. Formulas:  
ROI: NOIprice
Cash-on-Cash: cash flowcash invested
ROE: cash flowequity
2. Example: Property bought for $200,000 (with $50,000 down), NOI $20,000, cash flow $8,000, appreciated $30,000  
ROI: $20,000$200,000=10%

ROE: $8,000$80,000=10%
